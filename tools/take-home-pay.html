Questions

How accurate is this?

It uses projected 2026 US federal brackets and the 2026/27 UK rates with standard deduction and personal allowance baked in. State tax is a flat effective-rate approximation across all 50 states; Scotland uses its own income-tax bands. For tax filing use a qualified accountant; for forecasting it sits within a few percent of payroll.

Which states and UK regions are covered?

All 50 US states (with the eight no-income-tax states correctly zeroed out), plus England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland. Scottish residents see their own band structure; rUK residents share the 20% / 40% / 45% bands.

Does this account for 401(k), HSA, or pension contributions?

Not in this version. The calculator assumes no pre-tax deductions so the gross figure is what you and your employer agreed. To model 401(k) or pension across a full forecast, use the Pro scenarios planner.

Why a flat rate per state?

Most states use progressive brackets, but for a planning estimate a single effective rate is within ~1% of the bracketed figure for typical W-2 earners. We trade pinpoint accuracy for a clean comparison across all 50 states.

Is my salary data shared anywhere?

No. The calculation is pure JavaScript that runs on your device. Your salary never leaves the browser.
