## 2. WILLINGNESS-TO-PAY HYPOTHESES (the experiments)

You do not know what people will pay. Anyone who claims to know is lying. The job is to design experiments that *disconfirm* your guess as fast and as cheaply as possible.

### 2.1 Experiment 1 (ship first) ÔÇö "The ÔéČ9 Smoke Test"

**Goal:** Disconfirm that Pro at ÔéČ9/mo has any pull at all in the chosen ICP.

**What ships:**
- Pro page shows **ÔéČ9/mo or ÔéČ79/yr** (no founder discount visible yet)
- PayPal sandbox Ôćĺ swap to live for this test
- Single CTA: "Start 14-day free trial ÔÇö no card required"
- Trial endpoint that does NOT require payment up front (collects email + intent, then asks for card on day 14)

**Success metrics (the actual numbers):**

| Metric | Target | Kill threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Landing Ôćĺ trial signup | **Ôëą 4%** | < 1.5% over 200+ visitors Ôćĺ ICP wrong |
| Trial Ôćĺ paid conversion | **Ôëą 10%** | < 4% Ôćĺ product wrong OR price wrong, ambiguous |
| Card-add rate during trial (asked at day 7) | **Ôëą 25%** | < 10% Ôćĺ intent isn't real |
| First 100 visitors that even *click* the price page | **Ôëą 35%** of homepage visitors | < 15% Ôćĺ top of funnel positioning wrong |

**Sample-size note:** Below ~150-200 visitors you cannot distinguish signal from noise. If the channel test (section 4) doesn't deliver Ôëą150 unique landing visits, **the experiment is inconclusive, not failed.** Important distinction; founders confuse the two.

**Cost of running:** ÔéČ0 incremental (lives inside the channel-test budget).

**Duration:** 14 days, matched to the channel test.

### 2.2 Experiment 2 (if Exp-1 fails) ÔÇö "The ÔéČ5 Diagnostic"

**Triggered when:** Exp-1 trialÔćĺpaid conversion < 4% AND landingÔćĺtrial Ôëą 4%. (Meaning: people want the product but balk at ÔéČ9.)

**What ships:**
- Same product, same trial flow
- Pro page A/B: 50% see ÔéČ9, 50% see ÔéČ5
- Run for 7 days minimum, Ôëą75 visitors per arm

**What it tests:** Is ÔéČ9 a *price-elasticity* problem (cut price, conversions double) or a *value-perception* problem (cut price, conversions barely move)?

**Decision tree from Exp-2 outcome:**

| Outcome | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ÔéČ5 converts Ôëą2x ÔéČ9 | Price-elastic. People want it cheap. | NOT a green light to lower price. Means ICP is wrong (you've found the wrong-cohort). Move to ICP-2. |
| ÔéČ5 converts 1.2-2x ÔéČ9 | Mild elasticity, marginal customers. | Hold price at ÔéČ9, focus on value comms. |
| ÔéČ5 converts ÔëĄ1.1x ÔéČ9 | Inelastic. Price is not the blocker. | Product/feature gap. Go back to product. **Do not lower price.** |
| Both arms convert <2% | Channel/ICP problem, not pricing. | Stop testing pricing. Find new ICP. |

**Key insight:** Lowering price to "win" a failing experiment is the most expensive mistake a pre-PMF founder makes. It locks in a low-value cohort and trains the market to expect cheap. **Exp-2 exists to *diagnose*, not to set the new price.**

### 2.3 Experiment 3 (if Exp-1 succeeds) ÔÇö "Annual Push + Premium Test"

**Triggered when:** Exp-1 hits or exceeds targets.

**What ships:**
- Default the pricing page to **annual (ÔéČ79)** with monthly as the secondary option
- Add Premium (ÔéČ19/mo) as visible third tier
- A/B the founders' lifetime offer (50% see it, 50% don't) to test whether it accelerates conversion or just discounts existing buyers

**What it tests:**
1. Annual default capture rate (target: 30-45% of paid conversions go annual)
2. Premium attach rate (target: 5-15% of Pro converters upgrade or pick Premium directly)
3. Founders' offer lift (target: Ôëą1.5x conversion when shown vs not shown ÔÇö if <1.2x, kill the offer; it's giving away margin without driving incremental signups)

**Cost of running:** ÔéČ0 incremental.

**Duration:** 30 days from end of channel test.

### 4.2 Hard kill criteria

1. **Visitor Ôćĺ trial signup < 1.5%** over 200+ unique landing-page visitors Ôćĺ ICP wrong, change ICP not price
2. **Trial Ôćĺ paid conversion < 4%** with sample size Ôëą30 trial signups Ôćĺ product/price problem, run Exp-2

### 4.3 Founder opportunity cost ÔÇö the unquantified line item

Founder spending 14 days on GTM is **not free**. Loose quantification:

- Founder hour value (opportunity cost): ~ÔéČ50-100/hr based on (a) what the founder could earn contracting, (b) what comparable senior engineers cost in EU markets
- 14 days ├Ś ~6 productive hours/day (GTM is exhausting) = **~84 hours**
- Implied opportunity cost: **ÔéČ4,200-ÔéČ8,400** of foregone engineering output

**Is that worth it?** Pre-PMF, yes ÔÇö but only if the test is *designed to disconfirm*, not to "see what happens." The cost of 14 days of GTM with no falsifiable hypothesis is the same ÔéČ4,200-ÔéČ8,400, but you learn nothing. **The hypothesis is the entire value.**

What you lose by NOT engineering for 14 days: maybe one major feature, several bug fixes, polish on the Pro page. None of which matters if no one wants the product. **Order of operations is correct: GTM test first, polish second.**

## 6. ANTI-RECOMMENDATIONS

**What looks attractive at pre-PMF that I reject:**

1. **Raising any capital before ÔéČ5k MRR.** Anti-pattern. Investor money at zero traction sets a low valuation, dilutes the founder 20-40%, imports growth expectations that may not match the niche, and adds a board even if informal. Stay alone, stay cheap, stay nimble. Money buys nothing pre-PMF that effort doesn't already buy.
2. **Pricing by feature count ("Pro has 5 budgets, Premium has unlimited").** Anti-pattern. Customers don't buy features, they buy outcomes. Tier by *value delivered* (basic budgeting vs AI-powered insights) not by counting. Feature-count pricing trains buyers to nickel-and-dime you and makes every product decision a pricing-ladder problem.
3. **A free-forever Pro tier "to seed the funnel."** Anti-pattern. You already have a freemium model (free tier exists). Adding a free Pro tier dilutes the paid signal you desperately need. The 14-day trial is your funnel mechanism. Don't muddy it.
4. **Discounts at <ÔéČ9 to "fight off" Toshl/Buxfer.** Anti-pattern. You are not competing with Toshl on price; you're competing on positioning (privacy, EU, AI). Match price to the *anchor* you want buyers to use (YNAB/Lunchmoney), not the cheapest competitor.
5. **Pricing announcements made permanent.** Anti-pattern. Every price you publish today should be revisitable in 60-90 days. Use phrases like "Launch pricing" or "Founders' rate" in copy so buyers don't feel rugged when you raise. **Pricing is a hypothesis until you have 200+ paying users.**
6. **Lifetime deals beyond the founders' 100-seat cap (AppSumo-style).** Anti-pattern. Mortgages future MRR for one-time cash. The 100-seat founder cap is the maximum acceptable balance-sheet exposure. AppSumo or similar bulk-LTD deals beyond that are pre-PMF revenue cocaine ÔÇö feels great, kills you slowly.
7. **Hiring (anyone) before ÔéČ15k MRR.** Anti-pattern. Every euro of founder time is the cheapest engineering hour you'll ever have. Hire when revenue can absorb a ÔéČ50k+ EU all-in cost, not before.
8. **Spending more than ÔéČ250 on the 14-day test.** Already covered. Anti-pattern. ÔéČ1,000 ad spend tests the channel, not the product. You need to test the product first.
