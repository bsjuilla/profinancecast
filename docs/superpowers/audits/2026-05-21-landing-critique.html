5-Dimension Design Critique

ProFinanceCast landing — May 2026

A magazine-style fintech site with a clear editorial thesis ("the numbers your grandfather worked on a ledger — now computed in your browser"), strong photography, and a polish gap in the small things. The big moves work; the 90/10 details bleed the score.

Reviewer · OD · critique skill Date · 2026-05-21 Artifact · profinancecast.com (index.html, blog.html, ~10 sub-pages)

Five-axis read

Mean: 6.8 / 10 · band: strong with rough spots. Detail axis (5) is the asymmetric drag — every other axis is 7+. Polish those rough spots and the whole shape rounds out to a 7.5+ overall.
1 · Philosophy consistency· 哲学一致性
8/10
Strong

One declared direction — "editorial finance" / magazine aesthetic — and the site argues for it everywhere. Fraunces serif display + Inter sans body + ui-monospace meta + gold (#D4AF6A) accent ≤ 5%, used by the SAME rule across hero, steps, pricing, blog. The compass-on-paper photo (Step 02), future-value-formula paper, gold calligraphy flourish, hero-ledger image — all sit inside ONE visual vocabulary. The drift: dashboard chrome (when you sign in) is more "techy SaaS" than the landing's editorial register. Drops it from 9 to 8.

KeepSingle-photo-per-section discipline (Step 02 compass + ledger). Don't let the marketing team add a stock photo of "happy people pointing at laptops".
FixCarry the editorial photo aesthetic into the dashboard sidebar / topbar (or accept the register shift is deliberate and document it).
Quick winBring the gold-flourish hairline into blog post H1 row — currently only on landing.
2 · Visual hierarchy· 视觉层级
7/10
Strong

Hero h1 is unambiguously the loudest element on page 1 (clamp(48px, 7.6vw, 104px)). Section ledes use Fraunces serif at consistent sizes. Pricing cards mark the recommended tier with the "Most chosen" tag + gold border + tinted background (good). Drops from 9: the bento card 6 ("Encrypted by default") opens with a 120px photo instead of the 44×44 emerald icon tile that cards 1-5 use — visual rhythm breaks at row 3. Blog index article cards are all uniform — no eye direction, every card competes equally.

KeepHero h1 + italic accent ("not advice."). It's the most memorable element on the site.
FixBento card 6 photo → either wrap in 44×44 icon slot like its siblings, or move photo to footer block under the body.
Quick winOn blog index, make the featured card 2× wider (it's currently sized identically to siblings — the visual hierarchy says "featured" but the layout doesn't agree).
3 · Detail execution· 细节执行
5/10
Functional

This is where the polish gap shows. One audit pass found 9 visual bugs: STEP 02 legend text overlapping the optimistic legend line and the endpoint dot (index.html:1872-1878, fixed); scroll-padding-top missing on landing/blog/about so every anchor link scrolled section title under the fixed nav (fixed); pricing-card resting translateY(-4px) on the Pro card meaning the three CTAs landed at three different y-positions (fixed); .step grid using align-items:center while Step 02's left column had an extra figure, floating the chart mid-air relative to copy (fixed); blog article-grid SVG captions clipped 2px by overflow:hidden (fixed). Multiple CSS values that diverged by 1-4 pixels in sibling components. This isn't broken — it's tape-and-string. After today's fixes the score moves up; at audit time it's 5.

KeepThe pfc-tokens.css design-system file. Already enforces shared sizing for r, line, ink, gold, etc. Don't dilute.
FixCTA copy inconsistency — 6 different wordings for the same primary action ("Start free" / "Start your free forecast" / "Run the forecast" / "Get started free" / "Try ProFinanceCast" / "Use the calculator free"). Pick one.
Quick winFooter link styles — some /blog, some /blog.html. Pick canonical form, repeat across all pages.
4 · Functionality· 功能性
7/10
Strong

Sign-up → onboarding → dashboard flow works end-to-end. PayPal billing integrated. Pricing CTAs deep-link to auth.html?next=/billing.html#founders (well done). Anchor IDs all match nav targets — no dead href="#" links in the public surface. Blog filter chips work (lacking aria-pressed but visually functional). Drops from 9: <div class="field-icon" onclick="togglePw()"> in auth.html — not keyboard-accessible. Info pages (help/about/privacy/terms) lack a Sign-in + Start-free header, leaving visitors who land via search with no conversion path. CTA copy drift means analytics can't cleanly attribute clicks to a single button.

KeepDeep-link signup → billing flow. Reduces friction for users who decide on the pricing page.
FixAdd Sign-in + Start-free buttons to help / about / privacy / terms headers. Visitors from search drop without a conversion CTA.
Quick winAuth password-eye <div onclick> → <button type="button" aria-label="Show password">. Keyboard + screen-reader accessible.

7/10
Strong

"Editorial finance" as positioning is genuinely fresh — not another generic fintech SaaS look. The compass-on-paper / future-value formula photography is earned (matches the "your grandfather's ledger, your browser's math" thesis). "We compound forward — to the day" is a memorable framing. The hero copy "Your money's path through the next decade. Without one regret." has a literary register most fintech sites would never attempt. Drops from 9: the blog index drops the photo-rich aesthetic entirely (pure SVG chart stand-ins) — the editorial promise breaks the moment a visitor clicks "Blog". The dashboard, when you reach it, doesn't pick up the editorial typography (separate critique).
